Free Productized Service

The Admin Time Audit

30 minutes. 8 questions. A bespoke field guide they keep forever. You cross over into their world, understand their weight, then leave behind something genuinely useful — whether they ever hire you or not.

How to Open the Call
First 60 seconds
Your posture on this call: you are the neighbor who showed up. Not the expert who arrived. Listen more than you talk. Everything they tell you becomes the raw material for a gift you send within 24 hours — one they'll keep, reference, and probably show someone else.
Opening Script
Hey [Name], I'm really glad you made time for this — I know that's not easy when you're running a practice. Before we start, I just want to say again: this isn't a sales call. I'm here because I genuinely want to understand what your day-to-day looks like — the parts that are working and the parts that feel like they're always just slightly out of control. I'm going to ask you 8 questions. At the end I'll share my honest read on what I'm seeing, and within 24 hours I'll send you a personal guide — built entirely from what you tell me today — with one thing you can actually implement yourself this week, for free, plus a preview of what else is possible if you ever want to go further. You can do whatever you want with it. There's no ask at the end. Ready?
The 8 Discovery Questions
Listen twice as much as you talk
Every answer feeds the deliverable. Q7 and Q8 are especially important — Q7 reveals your Quick Win, Q8 gives you the emotional close for the guide. Take notes in their exact words.
01
Walk me through what happens the moment a new patient or customer first contacts you — from their very first message to when they walk through the door.
Maps the full intake flow — manual steps surface immediately when they describe it out loud
02
How do you handle appointment reminders right now — and what happens when someone no-shows?
No-show rate is a direct revenue leak and usually the fastest, most satisfying automation win
03
What does your intake or onboarding paperwork look like today — paper, digital, or somewhere in between?
Paper-based intake = immediate high-value automation opportunity, especially in healthcare
04
Roughly how many hours a week does your team spend on data entry or moving information between systems?
Quantifies the invisible weight — their answer becomes your ROI anchor in the guide
05
Do you follow up with clients after appointments or purchases — and if so, how does that actually happen day to day?
Follow-up automation is consistently the highest-ROI, lowest-friction starting point
06
What tools or software does your team actually open every single day?
Critical — tells you which free tools to build the Quick Win around so it feels native, not foreign
07
What's the task your team keeps doing that someone has quietly thought "there has to be a better way" — but nobody's had time to figure it out?
⭐ This answer becomes your Quick Win. The thing they name here is what you teach them to fix in the guide — step by step, free tools, runnable by Friday.
08
If I could give your team 5 hours back every single week — what would you actually do with that time?
⭐ This answer closes the guide. Their own words, reflected back — "that's what this is for." Makes the ROI personal and human, not theoretical.
